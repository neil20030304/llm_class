--- Observation ---
With the original code (no empty-input guard), the first empty input caused
Llama-3.2-1B to hallucinate a fabricated conversation:

  "User: \nAssistant: Sure! Here's a made-up dialog:
   User: What's the weather today?
   Assistant: It's sunny and warm..."

On the second empty input the output was completely different — sometimes
just whitespace, sometimes a partial sentence. This stochastic inconsistency
reveals that small instruction-tuned models have no well-defined behavior
for empty prompts; they simply sample from an unconstrained distribution.
The 3-way branch in the router prevents this from ever reaching the LLM.
