EXTENSIONS OF REMARKS
HONORING MAJOR MATTHEW N.
TOPITZER

HON. JOHN R. CARTER
OF TEXAS

IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES

Friday, January 16, 2026
Mr. CARTER of Texas. Mr. Speaker, I
proudly salute Major Matthew N. Topitzer for
his outstanding service to the United States
Army and for the remarkable professionalism
he has demonstrated throughout his military
career. Major Topitzer has consistently embodied the dedication, integrity, and commitment to excellence that define the finest officers in our Armed Forces.
Major Topitzer’s military journey reflects
broad operational experience and steadfast
leadership. He began his service as a Signal
Platoon Leader in the 2nd Combat Aviation
Brigade at Camp Humphreys, South Korea,
and later led paratroopers in the 112th Signal
Battalion at Fort Bragg. His expertise in communications and network operations took him
to Special Operations Command—Europe in
Stuttgart, Germany, and to Fort Belvoir, where
he served as a technical project’s leader for
the 1st Capabilities Integration Group. In 2020,
he deployed to Kabul, Afghanistan, serving as
Deputy CJ6 for NATO Special Operations
Joint Task Force—Afghanistan.
I had the privilege of working closely with
Major Topitzer when he served as my Defense Fellow through the Army Congressional
Fellowship Program. His thoughtful counsel,
analytical rigor, and deep understanding of defense policy were invaluable to my office. Following his fellowship, he continued his exemplary service as a Congressional Budget Liaison to the Assistant Secretary of the Army for
Financial Management and Comptroller, presenting key research, development, and intelligence portfolios before Congress.
Major Topitzer’s achievements also include
earning a Master of Legislative Affairs from
George Washington University and completing
advanced military education, including SERE
High-Risk, Airborne School, and the Interagency Communications Course. He further
demonstrates his commitment to service
through his role as a White House Military Social Aide.
It is an honor to commend Major Topitzer
for his distinguished service to the Army and
the Nation. I extend my warmest congratulations to Matthew and his wife Ana as they
continue this journey of service together.

HONORING CAPTAIN ISAAC
CAMACHO

DMwilson on DSK7X7S144PROD with REMARKS

HON. TONY GONZALES
OF TEXAS

IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES

Friday, January 16, 2026
Mr. TONY GONZALES of Texas. Mr.
Speaker, I rise today to pay tribute to Captain

Isaac Camacho, a true American hero whose
courage continues to inspire generations. Born
on June 3, 1937, in Fabens, Texas, he moved
to El Paso at the age of 13 after the passing
of his father. He graduated from Jefferson
High School in 1955 and immediately enlisted
in the United States Army, joining the Airborne
Infantry of the 11th Airborne Division. His
dedication and skill led him to the elite ranks
of the U.S. Army Special Forces, serving with
the 1st Special Forces Group.
Mr. Speaker, Captain Camacho’s service is
marked by one of the most extraordinary stories of the Vietnam War. On November 22,
1963, during his second tour, he and three fellow soldiers were captured by Viet Cong
forces at Camp Hiep Hoa. He endured more

Mr. TONY GONZALES of Texas. Mr.
Speaker, I rise today to recognize a distinguished public servant, veteran, and law enforcement leader—Sheriff Darrin Mitchell—
whose decades of service to our Nation and to
the people of Texas exemplify duty, courage,
and integrity.
Darrin Mitchell began his service to the
United States in January of 1985 when he enlisted in the United States Army as a Parachute Rigger. His first assignment placed him
among some of our Nation’s most elite warriors, serving with the 5th Special Forces
Group (Airborne). Initially stationed at Fort
Bragg, North Carolina, he continued his service when the unit relocated to Fort Campbell,
Kentucky. Mr. Speaker, during this period he
supported critical missions and deployed in
support of Operations Desert Shield and
Desert Storm from 1990 to 1991—two of the
most consequential military operations of the
modern era. His work ensured that America’s
airborne forces remained supplied, prepared,
and mission-ready in the harshest conditions.

CONGRESSIONAL RECORD — Extensions of Remarks

After receiving an honorable separation from
the Army in 1994, Darrin Mitchell continued
his commitment to public service by pursuing
a career in law enforcement. He attended and
graduated from the Permian Basin Regional
Police Academy in 1995 and that same year
joined the Winkler County Sheriff’s Office,
where he served with dedication until 2002.
His call to serve then brought him to the Department of Homeland Security’s Transportation Security Administration. From 2002 to
2009, he served as a Master Behavioral Detection Officer—one of TSA’s most specialized
roles—helping safeguard the traveling public
in the years following the 9/11 attacks.

Mr. Speaker, Sheriff Darrin Mitchell represents the highest ideals of service—first in
uniform defending our Nation, and later in law
enforcement safeguarding his community.
Today, we honor his enduring commitment
and his exemplary career of service to Texas
and the United States.

In 2009, he returned to the Winkler County
Sheriff’s Office, first as a Correctional Officer,
then as a Patrol Deputy in 2010. His leadership, professionalism, and investigative skill
led to his promotion as Chief Investigator in
2012. When the sitting Sheriff retired in 2016,
Darrin Mitchell stepped forward once again,
running for office and earning the trust of the
community he had served for so many years.
He was elected Sheriff of Winkler County and
has since continued to protect and lead with
distinction.
