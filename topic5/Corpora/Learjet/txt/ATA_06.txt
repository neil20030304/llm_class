                     STATIONS - DESCRIPTION AND OPEBATION

1.   General
     A.   The aircraft is divided into reference points along three axes. The refer-
          ence points are measured in inches. These reference points provide a
          means of quickly identifying the location of a bulkhead, component, etc.


     B.
          (1)   Fuselage station, water line and butt.ock line measurements used in
                this manual are rounded to the nearest inch.
          All reference points may be converted to metric measurement (centimeters)
                                                                                             1
          by multiplying the reference point (in inches) to 2. 54 •.
     C.   ~ following terms are used for reference points.


          FS - Fuselage station is a horizontal reference designation starting at the
                 nose of the aircraft.

          WL - Water lfne is a vertical reference designation measured parallel to
               the ground.

          BL - Buttock line ls a horizontal reference designation starting at the air-
               craft centerline. Right or left is added to indicate the direction from
               aircraft ~enterline (RBL & LBL).

          WS - Wing station is a horizontal ~eference designation s'f:a:rting ·at the wing
                 centerline, and measured perpendicular along wing datum.

          NAC STA - Reference points that apply to the engine nacelle.
