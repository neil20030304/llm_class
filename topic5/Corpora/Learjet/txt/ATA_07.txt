                       LIFTING - MAINTENANCE PRACTICES

1.    General
      A. Lifting (jacking) is accomplished by using conventional tripod jacks to
         raise the aircraft at three points. The nose or fuselage jackpoint is
         locatedjust aft of the nose wheel well. The·. wing jackpoints are lo-
         cated on the wing, just aft of the main landing gear.· Holes are pro-
         vided in the aircraft structure at the j ackpoints for installation of the
         jack pads. The jack pads and attaching parts are stored in the loose
         equipment kit in the baggage compartment.
      B. The entire aircraft may be lifted by wing and fuselage jack points to -
         perform landing gear tests, removal and installation of nose and main
         landing gear, leveling and for other major repairs.
      C. The aircraft should be on a level surface and protected from wind
         gusts, preferably in a hangar.

             EMERGENCY LIFTING -·MAINTENANCE PRACTICES

1.    General
      A. For emergency lifting, it may be necessary to use a sling or inflatable
         pneumatic bags for the initial lift to be followed up with conventional
         tripod jacks.

3•    Lifting Aircraft
      A. Sling Method (See figure 201)
           (1) Using a large phillips head screwdriver, remove two bolts from
               each wing ..
           (2) Attach wing lugs to wing using bolts P/N AN6-10A and attach
               cables to wing lugs andspreader bar.                         ·
           (3) Slip belt under aircraft to the position shown and attach
               cables to belt and spreader bar assembly.
           (4) Adjust chain ends to align spreader bar parallel with the aircraft
               W.L.      .

                CAUTION: AVOID IMPACT LOADING DURING LIFTING.

            (5) As the aircraft is lifted, watch center of balance. The chain
                adjuster may have to be repositioned to keep the aircraft from
                tipping.
            (6) Stabilization ropes should be attached to each wing tip and either
                the nose or tailcone for guidance.
            (7} The cabin doors should be closed and the airplane transported as
                smoothly as possible to prevent further damage.
