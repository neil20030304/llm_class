                GENERAL - DESCRIPTION AND OPERATION
1   Descri tion                '
    A Tﬁle hydraulic system supplies ﬂuid (MIL-H-5606) under pressure of
        approximately 1500 psi (1450 psi on Aircraft 25-181 and Subsequent)
        to the brake, landing gear, flap and spoiler systems.       .
        Hydraulic fluid for systems operation is supplied from a reservoir
        through hydraulic supply shutoff valves to the engine driven hydraulic
        pumps. The hydraulic reservoir is pressurized to approximately 10
        psi through a pressure line from the cabin and an aspirator located in
        the return line. On Aircraft 25- 181 and Subsequent the reservoir is
        pressurized to approximately 20 psi through a pressure line and pres-
        sure regulator from the bleed air duct.                           -
        The engine driven hydraulic pumps supply fluid through check valves
        and a filter to the hydraulically operated systems upon demand.
        An accumulator absorbs and dampens pressure surges, thus prevent-
        ing damage to the system. System pressure is maintained at 1200 "to
        1550 psi. If pressure increases to approximately 1700 psi, a relief
        valve opens and excess pressure is routed to the return system.
        The system is equipped with two direct reading pressure gages. One
        gage, located on the instrument panel, indicates. hydraulic system
        pressure forthe pilot and copilot. ‘ The second gage which is used by
        servicing personnel is located below the accumulator in the tailcone
        section and indicates accumulator pressure.
        Ground service quick-disconnects are provided for system pressuriza-
        tion and functional testing.                                '1‘   Y 9   _ ,     1
        The auxiliary hydraulic pump is plumbed to the hydraulic reservoir
        and electrically operated by the Hydraulic Pump Switch through a
        pressure switch. The pressure switch is plumbed to the ﬂuid side of
        the accumulator. The pump provides hydraulic pressure for inflight
        standby power.                    ~             '                  5

1   General
    A   General maintenance procedures should be thoroughly read and under-
        stood prior to performing any maintenance on the hydraulic system.
        Prior to removal of any hydraulic line, p fitting or component the
        hydraulic systemmust be depressurized as follows:
        (1) Relieve residual hydraulic pressure in lines by pumping brakes.
        (2) Lower tailcone access door and disconnect battery quick-discon-
              nects.                                         '
        (3) Loosen one of the hydraulic line nuts between the hydraulic reser-
              voir and the relief valve to bleed off reservoir" pressure. When
              pressure is released, tighten nut.
        (4)   Loosen nut on accumulator Shrader valve to bleed off air pressure.
            When pressure is bled off, tighten Shrader valve. 0           ,
        Prior to disconnecting any hydraulic fitting or component make provi-
        sions for catching fluid draining from fittings.     '   _
        Exercise extreme care to assure that all parts remain absolutely
        clean. A single grain of sand or particle of grit can cause internal
        failure of hydraulic components. Therefore, hydraulic lines and com-
        ponent fittings should be capped with clean caps immediately after re-
        moval. During installation of a hydraulic system component, the hy-
        draulic lines and component fittings should remain capped as long as
        possible.        A
        When installing 0-rings, keep mold flash of O-ring in one position to
        prevent twisting. When 0-ring is in its groove, make sure it is even-
        ly stretched around the circumference. Hydraulic cylinder glands and
        piston assemblies containing 0-rings must be carefully inserted with
        only enough pressure to overcome friction.
        Do not replace hydraulic system fittings indiscriminately. Identify
        fittings as they are removed to assure that replacement conforms with
        original installation.
        All fittings and B-nuts should be properly aligned before connection.
        Service the hydraulic reservoir and accumulator after installation of
        components. (Refer to 12-00-00)
        Check emergency air bottle charge and service if required.
        To expel air from the landing gear, flaps, or spoiler systems, cycle
        each system until air is expelled using an auxiliary power unit. Do

    1.     General                                             '       ~
     A     A.   The main hydraulic system consists of a ﬂuid -reservoir, two supply
                shutoff valves, two engine-driven pumps, check valves, an accumula-
                tor, a reservoir pressure regulator, two filters and four relief valves
2.         Reservoir                   p U                                           A
           A. The reservoir supplies ﬂuid to the aircraft hydraulic system. The
              unit is an airtight pressurized vessel consisting of a sight glass for
              servicing purposes and a filler plug attached to the filler neck. The
              filler neck contains an aluminum mesh screen to prevent fluid con-
            A tamination. The screen may be removed for cleaning by removing
         a    the filler cap and screen retaining nut.                           y
           B. Reservoir pressure is maintained at approximately 10 psi through an
              aspirator and a pressure line.to the cabin. On-Aircraft 25-181 and
              Subsequent,‘ reservoir pressure is maintained at approximately 20 psi

              Excess reservoir pressure is vented overboard through a relief valve.
              The reservoir pressure maintains a positive ﬂuid, flow to the engine
              driven pumps at high ﬂight altitudes.      -

      AUXILIARY HYDRAULIC PUMP - DESCRIPTION AND OPERATION

1.   Description
     A.     The auxiliary hydraulic pump is, installed on the hydraulic panel loca-
            ted in the tailcone section of the aircraft.
     B.      The electrically driven auxiliary hydraulic pump provides hydraulic
             system pressure for inflight standby power.
     'C.     The auxiliary hydraulic pump is controlled by the Hydraulic Pump
             Switch and cycled automatically by a pressure switch located in the
             system pressure line.                  W           I
2.   Operation (See figure 1)
                                                                  0




     CAUTION:         OPERATE AUXILIARY HYDRAULIC PUMP A MAXIMUM OF
                      3 MINUTES ON AND 20 MINUTES OFF TO PREVENT OVER-
                      HEATING AND BURNOUT or PUMP MOTOR.                             '
                                                       .    ,                    Q




     A.     With the Hydraulic Pump Switch set to HYD PUMP, an abnormal drop
            in hydraulic system pressure will actuate the pressure switch and
             energize the pump.                                              ¢            p
     B.      The auxiliary pump draws fluid from the bottom or reserve portion of
           ' the reservoir. This assures that fluid will be available even with low
            fluid in the reservoir.
     C.     When normal system pressure is restored the pressure switch will
            reset and deenergize the pump.
     D.      On aircraft equipped with pressure switch (P/N 730605), the switch
            will open at 14.00 (r50) psi and close at 1200 (i100) psi. On aircraft
